Muhammad Ali

A Star for the Greatest

Timeline of the legend Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942 - Born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. in Louisville, Kentucky, U.
  • 1954 - Made his amateur boxing debut against local amateur boxer Ronnie O'Keefe
  • 1960 - He made his professional debut, winning a six-round decision over Tunney Hunsaker. From then until the end of 1960, Clay amassed a record of 19-0 with 15 wins by knockout.
  • 1964 - Won the world heavyweight championship from Sonny Liston in a major upset at age 22. and later he announced that he no longer would be known as Cassius Clay but as Muhammad Ali.
  • 1967 - Married Belinder Boyd. After the wedding, she converted to Islam. The marriage later ends in divorce.
  • 1971 - "The fight of the Century" takes place between Ali and Joe Frazier in New York's Madison Square Garden. Frazier won by a unanimous decision in 15 rounds. The bout marked Ali's first defeat of his pro career.
  • 1974 - Ali and Frazier have a rematch in Madison Square Garden. This time, Ali won in 12 rounds.
  • 1975 - Ali took on Frazier for a third (and final) time, this time in the philippines. Ali beat Frazier in a TKO in the 14th round in what is forever known as "The Thrilla in Manilla".
  • 1978 - Ali loses to 1976 Olympic champion Leon Spinks in 15 rounds by a split decision.
  • 1981 - Retired from boxing and focused on religion, philantropinism and activism.
  • 2016 - Ali dies at age 74 in Phoenix, after being hospitalized with respiratory issues.
